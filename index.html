Part I

Why I chose this equation

I wanted to make my first equation cover as many of the properties as possible: a vertical and horizontal asymptote, an inflection point, and a local minimum.

I started out picking zeroes I liked (x = -2, 1, & 3) and expanded to form the numerator, then picked a vertical asymptote at x = 2 and a horizontal asymptote, so the denominator had to have x^3 as its highest degree and be equal to 0 at x = 2.

In retrospect, I should have picked round numbers for the maximums and minimums (or even the inflection point!) and then worked backwards to avoid Newton's Method, but I decided to stick with this function even when it required Newton's method.

Analysis

When starting to analyze this function, I was very scared to use Newton's method. But while doing this project, I learned it's a lot easier when you can type the whole function into your computer and press enter 10 times instead of having to painstakingly input the whole function into a calculator for each step.

Trying to design a function a function that included every necessary property reinforced the learning of what requirements a function needs to meet to have them, like what types of functions have a horizontal or vertical asymptote, minimums and maximums, points of inflection, etc.

Getting the derivative of this function really grew my confidence with derivatives, especially getting the second derivative, because it was quite a lot of steps to get this derivative.
