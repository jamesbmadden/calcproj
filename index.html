Why I chose this equation

I wanted to make my first equation cover as many of the properties as possible: a vertical and horizontal asymptote, an inflection point, and a local minimum.

I started out picking zeroes I liked (x = -2, 1, & 3) and expanded to form the numerator, then picked a vertical asymptote at x = 2 and a horizontal asymptote, so the denominator had to have x^3 as its highest degree and be equal to 0 at x = 2.

In retrospect, I should have picked round numbers for the maximums and minimums (or even the inflection point!) and then worked backwards to avoid Newton's Method, but I decided to stick with this function even when it required Newton's method.
